Toy Crossbow Project

This project was the first real project that I tackled from start to finish, and one of my most memorable at that. Our prompt was to simple: to make a toy for children, and the rest was up to our imagination. This was a big and quite open-ended task for a bunch of first-year engineering students with no concrete prior experience; it was all new to us, from the ideation and conceptulization, to sourcing materials, CAD, and prototyping, to finally manufacturing and writing our final report. All in all, it was a rewarding and valuable experience as the first of many projects to come.



Our toy was called the Sticky Bow: a crossbow-style toy with a motorized recall mechanicsm that allowed users to reel back projectiles after firing. The bullet was designed with a sticky front face that can attach to objects, allowing users to pull them back with the recall function. Inspired by Nerf guns, sticky hands, and crossbows, we combined the best features of each product to create a fun and innovative new toy!



The toy was built out of a PVC pipe as the main body, 3D printed parts to fit the recall mechanism, and wood for the trigger mechanism. The recall mechanism was a motorized system consisting of a custom 3D-printed reel, a manual clutch with friction plates and springs, and a housed motor inside of the PVC pipe. Once the projectile sticks to an object, pushing the recall button activates the motor, reeling both the bullet and attached object to the user.



The trigger mechanism is modeled after a crossbow, designed to securely hold and release the projectile with minimal force. Initially prototyped from aluminum, the final version was constructed from wood for increased performance and decreased mass. A guiding ramp was added to ensure a smooth and accurate launch.
